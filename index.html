Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know when my truck needs testing?

Your truck needs testing based on these criteria:

  • All heavy-duty vehicles with a GVWR >14,000 lbs operating in California must comply with periodic testing
  • Testing frequency depends on your vehicle's model year:
    • 2013 and newer vehicles: Every 6 months
    • 2007-2012 vehicles: Every 4 months
    • 2006 and older vehicles: Every 3 months
  • Additional testing is required if:
    • Your vehicle fails a roadside emissions monitoring test
    • You receive a citation from CARB
    • After major engine repairs or modifications

What happens during a CARB compliance test?

Our testing process includes:

  • Visual inspection of emissions control systems
  • OBD (On-Board Diagnostic) system check
  • Smoke opacity test
  • Comprehensive emissions testing
  • Documentation and reporting to CARB

What do I need to bring for the test?

  • Vehicle registration
  • CARB certification (if previously tested)
  • Maintenance records
  • EIN (Equipment Identification Number)
